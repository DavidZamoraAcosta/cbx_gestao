F10 Risco de contaminação da baseline AUD por Reservas Legais ▾ ⟳ EM ABERTO R1 · R2
📋 Finding (Verra)
The reference region (RR) does not differentiate Legal Reserves (LR) and non-Legal Reserves, and insufficient information is provided to assess whether it could lead to an overestimated baseline for the PA in instances that only consist of Legal Reserves. It is unclear how a project could combine APD and AUD activities over the same properties in Brazil without resulting in over-crediting (see Background Section). Part of the deforestation projected in the AUD project area (i.e., deforestation by the landowners up to the legal limit) is not expected to happen in the Legal Reserves and will not be avoided by the project.

Ação Requerida pelo VerraThe VVB must ensure the PP demonstrates - using verifiable and quantifiable evidence -- that historical deforestation in the RR was similar in LR areas and non-LR areas: If the above cannot be demonstrated, the VVB must ensure the RR is stratified and/or the spatial model adjusted to reflect the different deforestation risks in areas inside and outside LR (See background section). Alternatively, the VVB must ensure the AUD baseline model is not overestimated: The project design must be updated from a multiple project activity combining AUD and APD *to a single activity AUD project over the entire project properties;* OR Alternatively, the project is required to apply a conservative adjustment to the AUD baseline to avoid over-crediting i.e., ensure the model does not project deforestation by the landowners up to the legal limit while such deforestation is not expected to happen in the Legal Reserves and won't be prevented by the project (see background section). The VVB must ensure that Section 3.2.1 of the PD and Section 3.2.1 of the MR are updated to include the appropriate baseline GHG emissions calculations considering the updated baseline scenario/project design. The VVB must assess the revised PD and update the Validation report accordingly.

💬 Nossa Resposta (VVB)
The database for Sustainable Forest Management (SFM) in Rondônia state is available in Geoportal websites- The Reference Region contains some properties with Sustainable Forest Management (figure below); however, the data lacks important information such as date of license permit, whether the license is authorized or not, activation date, and end date of the SFM activity. The SFM dataset only provides the polygon boundary without any relevant additional information that could be used for estimating the deforestation rate analysis within the historical reference period and the correspondent SFM area. Given the current fragility of SFM dataset, a direct comparison between deforestation inside and outside SFM areas in the Reference Region during the historical reference period could be misleading or unreliable. Indeed, such an analysis would only be valid if SFM Areas could be paired with real-time deforestation events, enabling the relationship between the location of deforestation and the status of the Forest Management Area at the time of the deforestation event to be identified, which is not able in this case. Considering the issues discussed above, the available data on SFM Areas are not representative for the inclusion of a spatial variable in the model and possible stratification of the Reference Region. Since it is not possible to determine when the time period of SFM Area records, the current dataset represents only a snapshot in time --- one that may not be up-to-date --- and does not accurately reflect the configuration of SFM Area boundaries during the historical reference period. As a result, any comparison between historical deforestation dynamics and current SFM Area data is neither valid nor representative for a robust analysis.
